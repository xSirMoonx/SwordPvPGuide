General Tips for Reading This Guide

⚠️ IMPORTANT: The guide is organized in a way that makes sense. Follow the order of the channels. Don't proceed to the next section until you can perform the techniques in the current one comfortably. Leave Advanced and Advanced Sweeps until after you reach your peak. Core + Intermediate techniques are enough to nearly maximize your potential as a player.
💡 TRAINING TIPS
  • After being able to perform techniques intuitively, try to not overthink in PvP and play instinctively.
  • Always 1v1 better players than you or people that challenge you to improve.
  • Copy what better players do.
  • After you finish a fight, think on what you could have done better, and how you can counter certain strategies.
  • Don't play the same against everyone — change your playstyle depending on the player.
  • Hold sprint throughout all of the fight.
  • Aim straight forward for max reach.
  • ❌ Crouch does NOT reduce knockback like some think.

Hit Types

There are 5 different hits, all with individual sound.

🪃
SWEEP HIT
90% – 100%
Cancel your sprint hit, hit normally. Deals damage to everyone in range. Requires sprint cancellation.
💨
SPRINT HIT
90% – 100%
Hit while sprinting. Gains extra knockback on the opponent. Priority over standard hits, crits, and sweeps.
💥
CRITICAL HIT
90% – 100%
Hit while falling. Deals excess damage. Shows special particles. Most important offensive tool.
🔨
PICK HIT
0% – 90%
Under-charged hit. Less damage and knockback. Used situationally to stop KB or deal cheap damage.
🗡️
STANDARD HIT
90% – 100%
Hit while jumping without sprint. Not useful in combat on its own.
📝

Game Rules & Terms

📏
Reach
The max range is 3 blocks.
🚀
Momentum
Moving forward = more range. Moving back or standing in place = less range.
🔻
Knockback Rule 1
If you sprint while facing the target, you get less knockback (while falling too).
⚡
Knockback Rule 2
Hitting while taking KB and sprinting at the same time reduces your remaining KB by 40%.
🦆
Crouch
Decreases your hitbox size. Does NOT reduce knockback.
👑
Priority
Sprinthits have priority over Standard hits, Crits, and Sweeps.
First Hit
The first hit you land on your opponent when both are in a neutral position.
Hit Advantage
Whenever you are in a position where you can hit your opponent first.
Hit Priority
Initially gained through landing your hit first, giving you control over the fight. Can shift between players mid-trade depending on how things play out.
Stun
Getting a hit that gets your opponent stunned — unable to hit you back and move towards you.
Knock Away
A Sprinthit that knocks people away from you.
Double Hit
Whenever you knock someone away and are able to hit them again without getting hit back.
Crit Chain
Getting multiple crits in a row.
Stun Combo
Getting multiple stun hits in a row.

Core Techniques

⚔️ SPRINTHITS
Technique Creator: LoCr · Name Creator: LoCr
Stun
Risk ⭐☆☆ · Reward ⭐⭐⭐

🔻 How to Perform

Sprinthits deal more knockback and are meant to stun the target. If you hold sprint, only the first hit of the combo will be a sprint hit and all the rest will be sweep hits. You need to keep resetting your sprint to keep getting sprinthits with every hit. When the target gets knocked back, their range gets lowered, allowing you to keep comboing without being touched.

3 ways to reset your sprint:

  1. W-Taps — Release W and press it again after every hit. Riskier, countered by NoKb moves.
  2. Pausing W-Taps — Pause before pressing W again. Abuses full knockback, better stun.
  3. S-Taps — Best stun if done correctly. Keep s-tapping to 3 block range so opponent can't touch you.

Crouch-tap: Completely useless, but resets sprinthit.

You can get sprinthit combos with either strafe OR straight-line. Maintain 3 block spacing and perfect hit timing. When to use each sprint reset tactic is situational and depends on the spacing you need.

✅ Best ScenariosCombos
❌ Worst ScenariosVS Jump Reset, Hit Select

Old names: W Release Crits / Normal Crits · Creator: LoCr / Stumpie
Damage
Risk ⭐⭐☆ · Reward ⭐⭐☆

🔻 How to Perform

Jump and hit while falling. Release W every time before you go for a hit (to cancel the sprinthit) — this way your hit turns into a crit. You can start critting from the first hit or use it in the middle of a combo.

You can release "W" in different timings depending on the situation. Releasing W late gives a bit more range and KB reduction, but less control. Make sure you are jumping from the right distance or you can get knocked away before landing your hit.

⚔️ NOKB CRIT
Technique Inventor: Unknown · Name Creator: xYuvalx
NoKb
Risk ⭐⭐☆ · Reward ⭐⭐⭐

🔻 How to Perform

A counter to Crit Deflection. After you Crit someone, hold W and wait for your opponent to hit you. This way your knockback will be reduced. Only after they hit you, go for your 2nd hit — either a Punish Crit or a Falling Sprinthit (depends on situation).

  1. Punish Crit follow-up: Lets you stay on target and keep combo going without opponent deflecting your crits. Stop right in front of them (don't go through them) for consistent aim. KB still reduced. If opponent spaces well, you won't get many NoKb Crits in a row.
  2. Falling Sprinthit follow-up: After opponent sprinthits you, they get stunned (if done from right range), letting you keep the combo. Can s-tap after for extra space to ensure the stun. With correct spacing, you can stun someone no matter how close you land. Time your W key correctly — can let go of W briefly for more spacing.
✅ Best ScenariosVS Crit Deflection
❌ Worst ScenariosVS Double Crits, S-Hold Deflection, Repositioning, Standard Crits

⚔️ JUMP RESET
NoKb
Risk ⭐⭐☆ · Reward ⭐⭐☆

🔻 How to Perform

Jump while sprinting forward right as someone hits you. Must be done with perfect timing by knowing your opponent's hit timing. If done correctly, your knockback will be significantly reduced. Can also be spammable vs any sprinthit that isn't a "stun hit."

⚠️ Easily countered by:

  1. Delaying a hit intentionally to mess with the jump timing.
  2. Stun Hits: Against players good at spacing, Jump Resets will be BAD in many cases. Even if you eventually break free from a combo, you might take too much damage for it to be worth it.

When SHOULD you use Jump Resets?

  1. Learn when your opponent doesn't have enough distance to stun you with their next hit.
  2. Jump Resets can be good on first hits if both go for a ground hit sprinthit — reduces chances of getting stunned and can potentially help you stun them instead.
✅ Best ScenariosVS Sprinthits
❌ Worst ScenariosVS Perfect Stun Hits / Delayed Hit Timing

🔻 How to Perform

An advanced counter to Crit Deflections. Combo with NoKb Crit into Jump Reset.

Whenever you crit your opponent while they're on the ground, they will either try to deflect your crit, or punish crit/double crit after you crit them.

If opponent goes for Crit Deflection: Right after you land your Crit on them, use a Jump Reset on the incoming sprinthit. It will reduce your KB to literally 0. Makes it way harder to knock you away and puts you in a better position to out-trade.

⚔️ HOW TO SWEEP (BASICS)

Name Creator: LoCr. Sprinthits have priority over sweeps. To get a sweep you need to cancel the sprinthit first (or just not sprint):

1. Sprint Sweep — Hold "W" + Sprint and go for a combo. The first hit of the combo will always be a sprinthit, after which the rest of your hits will be sweeps until you reset your sprint.

2. "W Release Sweeps" — Release "W" every time before you hit. IE: Release "W" → Hit → Press "W" again. You could also strafe without touching "W" at all in some situations — usually better in the middle of a combo, don't start a combo this way.

Combos, Movement & Escape

🎯 COMBO SET-UP

Combos consist of Hit Timing and Spacing. If you space yourself around 3 blocks away while sprinthitting with correct hit timing (adjusts based on opponent's ping), you should be able to lock people in "stun combos" indefinitely.

Hit Timing: If you hit people too fast, their KB stops. If you hit them too slow, they can walk through your combo by just holding W.

Every single combo will start with a Stun OR a Knock Away on your opponent.

⚡ Winning a Trade VS Getting a Combo: When you win a trade, it's usually by 1-2 hits. Getting a combo lets you hit multiple times without taking damage. Therefore, look to COMBO when you get the chance, instead of attempting to win trades.

All ways to get a Stun / Knockaway to start a combo:

Repositioning, Crit Deflection, S-Hold Deflection, Falling Sprinthit after NoKb Crit, Falling Sprinthit, Uppercut, Hit Select, Pick Hit, Crit Trade Counters, Hit Dodging

🏃‍♂️ ESCAPING STUN HITS / COMBOS

There are 3 ways to escape stun combos depending on the situation:

  1. Hold S — This is the most common way to escape stun combos.
  2. S-Hold Jump — Jump while holding S. This will increase the knockback you receive. Use this ONLY when you feel like you need that extra knockback for them to not be able to reach you. Only works if done on hits like Deflection, that you can jump before they happen. Mainly useful VS high ping.
  3. Turn back completely — In some scenarios you will have to turn back completely and run to be able to get out of your opponent's reach.
🪃 HIT DODGING

1. Dodging First Hits: Walk back right before the opponent gets in range to hit you to dodge their incoming hit. Has to be done from the right distance with good timing. If you play defensively and let the opponent come to you, it makes it harder for them to hit you and easier for you to dodge. After dodging successfully, you can either attempt a stun combo or go for a crit.

⚠️ Countered by Falling Sprinthit!

2. Baits: Walk OR jump towards your opponent, baiting them to go in and then walk back immediately to dodge their hit, giving you a Hit Advantage. This is also the best counter to defensive players — getting them out of the defensive position, leaving them exposed to a hit.

3. The Turn Back Movement:

  1. When you see that your opponent is about to attack you, turn back away from your opponent for a second to dodge the hit, then turn back towards your opponent and attack (better going for a crit after turning back — it is more efficient).
  2. You can use this in the middle of a trade — sometimes you will have a very short period of time during a trade when you can use the turn back movement to dodge your opponent's hit or get yourself a hit advantage.
🚀 MOVEMENT

Movement can be used for making your opponent miss hits, or gaining distance necessary for a stun hit. Usually you wouldn't want to move around when there's a risk of getting stunned (straightlining decreases your knockback, reducing chance of getting stunned). Better to attempt it when close enough to not get stunned, OR when opponent misses a hit. Works best with crits and sweeps, but can also work with sprinthits.

Strafe Types:

  1. Short Strafe — Strafes with faster A/D movements
  2. Side-To-Side Strafe — Switch the strafing direction after every hit
  3. Circle Strafe — Strafe to one side, circling around the target

These techniques can be used with W-Taps, S-Taps, Pausing W-Taps, Crits, Uppercuts or Sweeps.

⚠️ Important: When you strafe it's a little harder to hit you, but you will also receive more KB which can result in getting stunned and combo'd. Only strafe in scenarios where you can't get punished for it. Strafing can be useful, but you could probably get tier 1 without strafing at all — it's a personal preference.
🥊

Playstyle Structure

⚠️ IMPORTANT: Your playstyle should always be flexible. When you fight players for the first time, there is going to be a safer approach. Once you learn their patterns and decision making, you can change your playstyle accordingly to beat what they do.
📋 GENERAL PLAYSTYLE TIPS
  • Focus on first hits. Make sure you either hit your opponent first, or at least at the same time as they hit you. Missing a first hit gives your opponent a chance to combo you.
  • You would usually want to lead with crits if possible. There are some situations where ground hits can be better, but ideally focus more on critting.
  • Avoid getting critted "for free" at all cost. Try to be the one who crits first and avoid getting hit by a crit while you're on the ground.
  • In some situations, when critting it's better to release W in the last second for max reach and taking the lowest knockback you can. Mainly good when going for a crit as a first hit, or doing NoKb Crits.
⚖️ WHEN THE FIGHT IS IN AN EQUAL STATE
1
If both are jumping in at around the same time, use Standard Crits. You want to be the first one to pull off a counter and gain a Hit Advantage, Hit Priority or a Stun.
2
Find a window to S-Crit, Deflect, use a Crit Trade Counter, or anything else that fits the situation best.
3
Be ready to hold S in case your opponent manages to properly deflect you mid-trade, to avoid taking extra damage.
⚔️ WHEN YOU'RE THE AGGRESSOR
1
Lead with a Crit. Now you have two options:
A
Going for Standard Crits: If they do ANYTHING that isn't a Crit Deflection, you should win the trade. BUT if they choose to Deflect, react fast and hold S to avoid getting countered.
B
Going for a NoKb Crit: If they try to Deflect it normally — you win. You have full control and can choose a Falling Sprinthit Followup or keep NoKb Critting. BUT: 1) They can lead with an S-Hold Deflection, which is very hard to react to in time. 2) They can lead with a Double Crit, which evens out the damage.
2
If you lead with a ground hit, it should mainly be when you're either too close to crit, or you're trying to catch your opponent off guard (e.g. straightline sprinthitting them when they're mid-air trying to crit you).
🛡️ WHEN YOU'RE THE DEFENDER
1
Avoid as much damage as possible whilst trying to get a Hit Advantage. Use Hit Dodging — it allows you to achieve a first hit, giving you control over the fight. Playing for hit dodging requires a more defensive position, making it harder to hit you. You can also jump in as soon as they get near.
2
When you get critted on the ground, lead with either a Crit Deflection or an S-Hold Deflection (NOT a Double Crit — too risky since you can't know if they'll go Standard or NoKb Crits).
3
S-Hold Deflection → When opponent lands far away (mid-long range). Ensures both Standard Crits AND mid-long range NoKb Crits get knocked away. ⚠️ BUT if used from close range, it could give opponent enough distance to stun you with a Falling Sprinthit. If they land close — FORCED to use Crit Deflection.
4
If you go for a regular Crit Deflection as they go for a short range NoKb Crit, use a "Double Crit" on your next hit to counter their next NoKb Crit. If they choose to Deflect it to counter you, hold S in time — it resets the fight and gets you out of that bad spot.
❓ WHAT HAPPENS AS YOU FIGHT THEM MORE?

When you reach a point where you learn how they play, you can start changing the structure mentioned above. For example, you CAN go for a Double Crit on your first hit if you already KNOW that their playstyle is revolved more around NoKb Crits, to be able to immediately counter it.

✅ You can pretty much change anything up depending on the player. Learn their playstyle, learn the way they think, and adapt properly.
🚀 SITUATIONAL COUNTERS TO DEFENSIVE PLAYERS
  • Play defensively like they do — it kills their advantage and is a safe way to read your opponent before choosing what to do.
  • Critting defensive players is harder because you can't crit from the normal range. Run in a straightline to them and crit from a closer range than you usually would. Also release "W" a bit later (right before the hit) for a max range crit.
  • Do a double jump to close the gap faster. Can be good but might also be a bit risky at times.
  • Sometimes catching them off guard with a sudden sprinthit, uppercut, or falling sprinthit when they don't expect it can be effective.
📋 Final Thoughts: Being too calculated in PvP could hold you back to an extent. Only use this specific structure IF: you've learned the techniques but still feel like you don't understand what you're supposed to do, you already have a solid playstyle but feel like you have weaknesses, or you feel like you've reached a peak and want to make sure you're doing things correctly.
🧑‍🏫 ADAPTATION

Every player has a different playstyle, different ping, and likes to go for certain moves more than others. Adaptation is all about changing what YOU do to counter what your opponent does. This requires a lot of PvP knowledge and experience.

  • Simple Adaptation: Adapting to a specific move your opponent goes for. For example, if you notice your opponent goes for NoKb Crits every chance they get, start instantly going for double crits whenever you get critted by them.
  • Advanced Adaptation: Recognizing a pattern of moves your opponent keeps going for — trading patterns, movement patterns etc — and knowing how to adapt and counter it.
  • Playstyle Adaptation: Changing parts of your playstyle, or changing it entirely, to beat a specific opponent by adapting to their playstyle.
🧑‍🏫 BAITS

Baiting your opponent into going for a certain move, then countering it. For example, baiting someone to go forward by running into them and then backing away to make them miss, giving you a hit advantage. Or jumping as if you're gonna crit them which baits them into trying to deflect, and then use a falling sprinthit instead to knock them away.

Special Matchups

🗡️ VS AXE 🪓

When using a sword against an axe, you should use any attack that has low KB. Sweeps and crits are extremely effective against axe users, both if they run away or if they try to trade.

  • When they try to trade — use punish crits and normal crit spam methods to out damage them.
  • If they try to run — chase with crits and sweeps.
  • Dodging their first axe crits is very important as it can really mess them up because they have to go through a huge cooldown.
  • Hold W to perform sprint crits and sprint sweeps — this is a very easy method of beating axe, you will out damage any axe user this way in an OP kit setting.
  • Combine both methods of dodging arrows and fighting axes if you are fighting a trident!
🗡️ VS SPEED 👨‍🔬

Speed users are very tricky to play against if you have no speed. The best way to handle a speed user is to play defensive and make them come to you, then you can get combos and crits on them easier than if you go to them instead.

💡 Play carefully against a speed user!

Ping

⚡ LOW PING

Control: You have a lot more control — can get stuff done with better timing and pull off smooth combos. You also get comboed more easily.

Benefits: Benefits good players more since good players mix up a lot of different techniques that would be unusable with high ping. Good players don't miss many hits and have good timing so it's harder to combo them anyway. Therefore good players will perform better the lower ping they have.

🐌 HIGH PING

Control: You have a lot less control — harder to land hits, worse timing. Delayed KB also makes it harder to aim on you or combo you and limits you to only certain techniques since most simply won't work with high ping.

Benefits: Benefits worse players more since it allows you to get away with missing hits and not get punished. Also allows you to perform well while having bad PvP knowledge or doing things incorrectly. Bad players will be a lot better with high ping.

📏 Fair Ping Range: If the ping is between 1–100ms, the fights are almost completely fair. From 100+ it starts becoming awkward.
📈 HOW TO USE LAG

As you move with lag, your character model and hitbox have delay server side that makes you further in front or behind than you are on your own screen. This is how "lag reach" is created.

When playing with 100+ ms, spamming crits and using S-tap combos is the most effective way to play. S-tap combos are easily done in high ping and they abuse ping reach. Crits are easily abusable as you take low KB, delayed KB, or teleport.

📉 HOW TO FIGHT LAG

The best thing you could do is try to adjust your timing to their delay.

Also, it's a lot more crucial to not miss first hits VS a lagger — you can get punished a lot harder than you normally would.

Lastly, trying to win trades is more consistent VS laggers than trying to stun them.
